The Lamu Cultural Festival.

Held annually in November, the Kenyan people come together Lamu comes alive with the celebration of past and future traditions and beliefs integral to the community. The event is rife in exciting cultural and modern activities such as henna painting, Swahili poetry, dhow sailing and donkey races. It is culminated with a traditional wedding and diverse Swahili dishes. The festival is an opportunity to experience the traditional beliefs of the community and learn about their rather peaceful lifestyle.

The Mombasa Carnival.

Dabbed the most popular in Kenya, the carnival features different Kenyan traditions and ethnicities. Held in November, the carnival features music, dance, parades and creative floats. A bust of color and diversity, the carnival represents both the African and Arabian cultures in a unique way. In addition, the carnival is a stop shop for souvenirs and a chance to enjoy the different Kenyan pallets that line the streets of Mombasa.

International Camel Derby Festival.

The camel derby is a spectacle unlike any other that is packed with a variety of events. It is held every year in the northern region of Kenya in Samburu. The derby, bursting with culture and competition brings adventure to the unharmed wilderness of Kenya. The derby is an action filled event that unites culture, diversity and adventure.
